engagements I once more steered for dear London and left Winchester in the middle of October to see what was going forward in the great world but not with likelihood of any continuance there that year of our Lord On the day of my departure Lord Tavistock honoured me with a present of fruit in a hamper well packed I set off highly gratified with the gift but much more so from the very flattering kindness of the compliment than by the value of the donation and to prove how good events are sometimes produced by trifles as once on a time the cackling of geese saved a city so I on my arrival in London judged that this hamper of Bury pears c would be a genteel and very acceptable present to Mr Rich and more so as the fruit would keep with care for some weeks at that season of the year and I added to the said hamper a very fine hare In London these things are esteemed presents but in Yorkshire I am under such constant obligations to  my friends that I have not the least reason to regret I
